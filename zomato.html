10

Break-even Achieved Globally
By 2018, Zomato had completed a decade of operations and achieved break-even across global segments.
Competition in the Indian food delivery market increased rapidly, with major players expanding their delivery networks aggressively.
Zomato now faced a critical strategic decision: how to sustain growth while remaining competitive in a crowded market.
The company needed to weigh domestic consolidation against global ambition — a classic growth dilemma for a scaling startup.

12 · Problem Space

Key Challenges
Facing Zomato

⚔️
Intense Competition
Swiggy's rapid expansion and UberEATS's deep pockets intensified competition for market share across Indian cities.
💸
Rising Logistics Costs
Delivery infrastructure expansion, driver incentives, and fuel costs dramatically increased per-order delivery expenses.
🏷️
Discount Dependency
Heavy reliance on promotional discounts and promo codes to attract and retain customers eroded contribution margins.
🌐
International Overstretch
Operating across 24 markets created financial pressure with high investment requirements and complex operational management.
13 · Framework

SCQA Framework

Situation
Zomato expanded from a restaurant discovery platform into a global food delivery company over 10 years, achieving international presence across 24 markets.
S
Complication
Competition intensified rapidly while operational costs climbed. Logistics expenses, discount dependency, and international overstretch created mounting financial pressure.
C
Question
How can Zomato sustain growth while maintaining profitability in a hyper-competitive market with razor-thin margins?
Q
Answer
Zomato must decide whether to focus on domestic market leadership, pursue selective global expansion, or adopt a balanced hybrid strategy.
A

15 · Recommendation

Strategic Options
& Recommendation

A
Domestic Focus — Strengthen India leadership
B
Global Expansion — Expand internationally
C
★ Balanced Strategy — India-first with selective global expansion
Recommended
Balanced Approach
🏆
Domestic Leadership
Deepen market penetration in India's Tier 1 and Tier 2 cities. Invest in loyalty programs, restaurant partnerships, and hyperlocal delivery infrastructure.
🌐
Selective Global
Retain presence in high-margin international markets only. Exit markets with poor unit economics; redeploy capital to higher-ROI opportunities.
⚙️
Operational Efficiency
Invest in technology and logistics optimization to reduce per-delivery cost. Move away from unsustainable discount-driven growth models.
📈
Diversified Revenue
Scale Hyperpure B2B business and Zomato Gold subscription revenue to reduce dependency on thin delivery margins.
